Business case
Problem statementUnderwriters spend 45 minutes manually assessing each D&O submission against appetite guidelines. The process is inconsistent across the team and creates a backlog of 200+ submissions per week. An AI triage agent would classify each submission as in-appetite, borderline, or out-of-appetite before underwriter review.
Expected benefitsReduce triage latency by 40%, improve consistency across underwriters, free senior capacity for complex cases.

Adverse action risk
This system produces eligibility decisions affecting whether applications are accepted or declined. Under CO SB21-169 and NAIC AI Bulletin, adverse actions require a human-reviewable explanation. The HITL strategy (underwriter reviews all outputs) satisfies this requirement, provided the override mechanism is logged.
medium risk
Fairness & bias exposure
D&O classifications based on SIC code, years in business, and loss history could correlate with protected-class-adjacent proxies. A fairness analysis across SIC segments is required before champion promotion. Appetite guide must be reviewed for discriminatory exclusions.
medium risk
Model drift & monitoring
Appetite rules change quarterly. The model must be monitored for drift against current guidelines. A validation run cadence of monthly, or on any appetite policy update, is recommended. Incidents should be raised if HITL override rate exceeds 15%.
low risk
Data residency & privacy
Submission data does not include personal identifying information beyond named insured entity name. No individual-level data is used. No cross-border data transfer. Privacy impact: low.
