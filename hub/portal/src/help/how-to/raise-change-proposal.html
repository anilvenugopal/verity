How to Raise a Change Proposal

Propose a risk reclassification or business change to an approved intake.

When to raise a change proposal

A change proposal is required whenever a material change occurs to an approved AI use case. There are two kinds:

  • Risk reclassification — the system's risk profile has changed: it now affects a different population, uses different data, or operates at a higher autonomy level. This triggers a re-assessment and may change the risk tier and associated obligations.
  • Business change — an operational change that does not alter the risk tier: a new model version, vendor change, or documentation update. The existing tier and obligations remain.

If you are unsure which kind applies, choose risk reclassification — it triggers the fuller review process and errs on the side of caution.

Step 1 — Raise the proposal

Navigate to the approved intake and click Raise Change Proposal. Select the kind and provide a rationale explaining what has changed and why the proposal is necessary.

Step 2 — Quorum sign-off

The proposal enters a quorum queue. Required approvers are determined by the intake's tier — the same roles that approved the original intake. Separation of duty applies: you cannot approve a proposal you submitted.

Step 3 — What happens on approval

  • Risk reclassification — linked registry assets are forked: existing versions are preserved and new versions are created. The intake's assessment is re-opened for revision and obligations are re-derived at the new tier. Forked assets must go through the promotion gate again once obligations are re-resolved.
  • Business change — the intake status is updated with the change record. Existing obligations remain; no re-assessment is required.

Rejection

If the quorum rejects the proposal, the intake remains in its current approved state unchanged. The rejection is recorded in the intake's audit trail with the approver's rationale.
