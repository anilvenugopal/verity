How to Resolve Obligations

Record evidence for compliance controls and raise exceptions where needed.

Step 1 — Open the Risk & Obligations tab

Navigate to the approved intake and click the Risk & Obligations tab. The platform lists all compliance obligations derived from the intake's risk tier and governance domains. Each obligation shows the canonical requirement it maps to, the required control, and its current status.

Step 2 — Record evidence

For each obligation, click Record Evidence. Enter the control code (pre-filled from the obligation), a description of how the control is satisfied, and an optional source reference — for example, a link to a policy document, test results, model card, or review record.

Evidence is append-only. You can add multiple evidence entries per obligation; the most recent one becomes the current record.

Step 3 — Raise an exception if a control cannot be met

If your team cannot satisfy a control as specified, raise an exception. Provide:

  • Compensating controls — what you are doing instead to mitigate the risk.
  • Rationale — why the standard control cannot be met and why the compensating control is sufficient.
  • Expiry date — exceptions are time-limited. The platform will flag the exception when it approaches expiry.

Exceptions require approval from a Compliance Officer or Risk Manager depending on the tier. The approver reviews the rationale and either approves or rejects it.

Step 4 — Resolution

Once every obligation has either recorded evidence or an approved exception, the intake's compliance status updates to resolved. Registry assets linked to this intake can now advance beyond staging to challenger and champion lifecycle stages.

If an exception expires before a permanent control is in place, the intake reverts to unresolved and linked assets are blocked from promotion until the exception is renewed or evidence is recorded.
